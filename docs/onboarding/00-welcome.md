# Welcome to Marro, Ethan

Welcome aboard. This packet exists to get you from zero context to genuinely useful, without needing any technical background. You don't need to know how to code, read code, or understand the engineering to be a strong contributor here — this guide is written in plain language, and any unavoidable jargon is defined the first time it appears and again in the glossary.

## What Marro is, in one sentence

Marro is a simple money app for medical students that answers two questions they can never get straight answers to: **"how long will my money last?"** and **"what will this degree really cost me?"**

## A few things worth knowing up front

- **It's real and it's live.** The app exists and runs on the internet today at **joinmarro.com**. It is early, but it is not a slide deck.
- **The founder is Mohamad Hijazi**, a medical student who built Marro because he personally needed it. (More on the team in doc 04.)
- **The guiding belief:** at this stage the scarce resources are *founder time, trust, and focus* — not fancy technology. Marro wins by being trustworthy and genuinely built for med students, not by being clever.
- **When something here says "TBD — confirm with Mohamad,"** that means the answer isn't written down anywhere I could verify, so please treat it as an open question to raise with him rather than an established fact.

Take your time with doc 01 and doc 02 — they're the foundation. The roadmap in doc 03 will make far more sense once you know what the product does.
