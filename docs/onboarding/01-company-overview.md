# Company Overview

## What Marro is

Marro is a **financial companion app for medical students**. Think of it as a calm, trustworthy money guide made specifically for people going through med school — not a generic budgeting app, and not a bank.

The product goal, stated in one line by the team, is:

> A 3-tab app a med student sets up in about 5 minutes and touches roughly 1 minute a month, that answers: *how long will my money last* and *what will my degree really cost me.*

That "5 minutes to set up, 1 minute a month to maintain" target is deliberate and important — it shapes almost every product decision.

## The problem it solves

Medical students are in an unusual and stressful financial situation:

- They borrow **very large amounts of money** (student loans) to pay for school and living costs.
- They typically have **little or no income** for years.
- The **financial aid letter** a school hands them is a wall of numbers written for the aid office, not for the student. Nobody translates it into "here's what you actually have to live on each month" or "here's what you'll really owe when you graduate."

So the core problem: a med student gets one big, intimidating number and no plan. Marro's job is to hand back **one number that actually matters** — how much they have to live on, how long it lasts, and what it all adds up to at graduation.

The app's own landing page puts it plainly: *"The aid office gives you a number. Nobody tells you what to do with it."*

## Who it's for

- **Primary audience:** medical students (people pursuing an MD or DO degree — the two kinds of doctor degrees in the US).
- It started **specific to one school** (Weill Cornell Medicine, abbreviated WCM — where the founder studies) and has since been made **school-agnostic**, meaning any US med school student can use it. The app already includes the full list of US MD and DO schools so a new user can pick theirs.
- Longer term, the plan is to grow **school by school**, building up a cluster of users at each school rather than spreading thin everywhere at once.

## Business model (how it might make money)

Marro is **free for everyone right now**, on purpose — the current priority is growth, trust, and learning whether people actually find it valuable, not revenue. The plan for making money later is written down but not yet in effect:

- **Long-term plan:** a paid subscription. The rough shape is a 2-week free trial, then $0.99 for the first month, then about **$5/month**. Early users are intended to be "grandfathered" — kept free or discounted forever as a thank-you.
- **The AI advice features** (see doc 02 and 03) are the main thing a paid tier would unlock more of.
- **Other possible revenue**, always designed so the *student also benefits*: sponsored opportunities (companies wanting to reach future doctors), referrals to financial products through properly licensed partners, and a deals/discounts marketplace. Any such content is always clearly labeled as a partner/sponsored offer, never disguised as Marro's own advice.
- **A firm rule:** money-making never contaminates the advice. Marro's guidance is never for sale.

A note on data: Marro's written rules say it **never sells your personal info**, and that individual records (your own debt, budget, numbers) are never sold or shared. The only data that could ever be used more broadly is *true aggregates* — group statistics where no single person can be identified (for example, "how students at your school compare"). This distinction is taken very seriously and is a core trust promise.

## Current stage

Marro is an **early-stage startup**, effectively pre-launch to the general public:

- The app is **built and live** at joinmarro.com. A meaningful amount of product already works (see doc 03).
- It is **not yet open to strangers at scale** — it's in a controlled phase while final trust and sign-up pieces are put in place.
- There is **no outside funding, no formal company entity yet, and no revenue** described in the documents. Forming an official company (a Delaware C-corp) is planned for when a committed cofounder comes on and cash/equity actually change hands — which is directly relevant to you.
